The Birth of Amul

In 1946, we, the dairy farmers of Anand, Gujarat, formed a cooperative to break free from exploitative middlemen. Led by Tribhuvandas Patel and later Dr. Verghese Kurien, we established Amul. Our cooperative ensured fair milk prices and prioritized quality. Our efforts led to the White Revolution, making India a top milk producer and turning Amul into a symbol of our empowerment and success.

The Amul Model

The Amul model is based on a three-tier cooperative structure:

1. Village Cooperative Societies (VCS): At the grassroots level, individual dairy farmers form cooperatives in their villages. These societies collect milk from their members, ensure quality control, and pay farmers fair prices based on the milk's quality and quantity.

2. District Cooperative Milk Producers' Union: Village cooperatives within a district are federated into a District Milk Union. This union processes the collected milk into various dairy products and markets them under the Amul brand. It provides technical support, veterinary care, and feed to the village cooperatives.

3. State Cooperative Milk Federation: At the state level, district unions form a State Federation, such as the Gujarat Cooperative Milk Marketing Federation (GCMMF). This federation coordinates marketing, branding, and distribution of products, ensuring a broad market reach and brand recognition.

This three-tier structure ensures efficiency, quality control, fair pricing for farmers, and effective marketing and distribution of dairy products.
